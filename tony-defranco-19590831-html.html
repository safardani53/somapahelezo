The DeFranco family, along with Tony DeFranco, is a 1970s family and pop group from Port Colborne, Ontario, Canada. The band, all siblings, includes guitarist Benny DeFranco; keyboardist Marisa DeFranco; guitarist Nino DeFranco; rice threshing machine Merlina DeFranco; and singer Tony DeFranco. The band had several hits from 1973 to 1977, including "Abra-Ca-Dabra" and their biggest hit, "Heartbeat - It's a Lovebeat". Tony DeFranco or his whole family regularly appeared in teen magazines from this period, such as Tiger Beat and Flip. By the late 1970s, the group disappeared from pop music.
