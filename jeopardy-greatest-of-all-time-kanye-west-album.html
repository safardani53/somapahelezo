Jeopardy! Greatest of All Time geniuses stumped by Kanye Wests latest album name Jesus Is King

THE apparent Jeopardy! "geniuses" were left stumped on Tuesday - by a question about a hugely popular Kanye West album.

Jeopardy! Greatest of All Time competitors Ken Jennings, 45, Brad Rutter, 41, and James Holzhauer, 36 were amusingly left in silence when host Alex Trebek, 79, begged the question of which 2019 album from the famous rapper contain the tracks "Follow God" and "Closed On Sunday”.

The answer - which was chosen from the category “Albums” - was worth $800.

Fan-favorite Ken threw up his hands completely stumped as the entire audience laughed at the entertaining missed opportunity.

“What is, Jesus is King?” is the question the beloved TV host was looking for that would have made the contestants that much closer to winning the competition.

People online immediately began to poke fun of the now-iconic Yeezus blunder.

“Kanye’s gonna be furious when he finds out none of the guys on Jeopardy had heard of Jesus is King,” one Twitter user joked.

“There is something absolutely incredible about three of the brightest men of our time not being able to name a Kanye album,” a watcher of the show said.

Another viewer wrote: “Ken is all like “What makes you think WE would know anything about Kanye?!”

“Somehow not surprised none of these nerds listen to Kanye” a fan of the show said.

This match is the fourth of the series, which will conclude when one of the players has won three rounds.

So far, Ken has championed two rounds and James has won once.

Brad has yet to be added to the scoreboard for the nail-biting race to a million dollars.

Ken - who is the highest-earning American game show contestant of all time - also holds the record of longest winning streak on Jeopardy! with 74 wins in 2004.

He has won over $2.5 million on the popular trivia show.

Professional sports gambler James skyrocketed to fame last year and followed closely behind his inspiration when he pocketed just under $2.5 million over a 32 game streak in 2019.

However, Brad holds the title for highest-earning Jeopardy! contestant of all time and has already cashed in more than $4.6 million from the show - nearly double his fellow competitors.

The winner of Jeopardy! The Greatest of All Time will be awarded $1 million.

The other two contestants will each bring home $250,000.
