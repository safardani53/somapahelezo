Kelly Nash got inspired by the former co-host of the morning show, Good Morning America, Charles Gibson to pursue a career in TV broadcasting. Should she want to look for a husband, Charles ought to be the logical reference.

The Sports presenter, age 27, is a host of MLB Network’s nightly highlight show, Quick Pitch. During NHL season, she serves as a studio host for NHL Network, and till this date, she has covered many players, including Clevland Indian's Chris Archer, Dodger's Corey Seager, and much more.

Born on 22 October 1990, Kelly grew up with her sisters, Kaitlyn, and Carolyn in South Carolina, USA.

Kelly Nash Bio: Joins Fox At Just 22 Years Of Age

After receiving a bachelor’s degree from Clemson University in Communication and Media Studies in 2006, she enrolled in the master’s programme, Youth Development leadership offered by the same university. Her pursuit of the master's degree in TV Broadcast Journalism took her to the University of Miami.

Between 2005 and 2010, she interned for WLPG covering South Florida Sports teams: Dolphins, Heat, Marlins, and Hurricanes.

The NHL presenter began her career in TV working for ACC Digital Network. At the tender age of 22, she made the high profile move to FOX Sports Florida in March 2013. At FOX Sports, she worked as a sideline reporter covering NBA’s Orlando Magic, MLB’s Tampa Bay Rays and Tampa Bay Lightning.

In November 2015, she replaced American sportscaster Lauren Shehadi at MLB Network as a host of The Rundown on MLB Network 

The native daughter of Carolina makes a little above $35,000 annually in salaries and bonuses.
