Who is American Idol's Margie Mays and where is she now?

AMERICAN Idol is a singing competition series that has introduced the world to some of today's biggest stars.

Among the previous contestants is Margie Mays who fans met during season 17 and are now curious to know what she is up to now.

Who is Margie Mays?

Margie Mays is known as a singer, songwriter, performer, and host, who became a household name following her run on American Idol.

"Singer actor host maniac, lover of a high pony," her Instagram bio reads.

Following her run on season 17, she then returned again the following season for another chance at the American Idol dream.

Prior to her American Idol journey, the Wilmington, Delaware, native obtained a degree in Human Biology and Communication from Stanford University, before heading to Los Angeles, where she later graduated with honors from the Los Angeles College of Music.

Where is she now?

After her journey on American Idol was completed, Mays went on to teach voice and piano lessons, according to her bio.

She is also the host of The Margie Mays Show where she interviews American Idol alums about their experience on and off the show.

Mays also has an active YouTube channel where she posts cover songs and vlogs about her life to her 35,000 subscribers.

What place did Margie Mays finish in?

During season 17, Mays performed All About That Bass by Meghan Trainor during the Hawaii Round, however, she did not advance past that performance.

Despite being eliminated, Mays did return that season for the finale where she sang This Is How We Do It alongside Montell Jordan, Austin Michael Robinson, and VoKillz.

The following year, in April 2020, Mays was eliminated again but this time, during Hollywood Week, ultimately finishing in the top 40.

Following her elimination, judge Katy Perry was slammed for "hating" on her and her then-boyfriend, Jonny West.

“It’s great to have a partnership but just know your priorities right now because you both are BARELY passing on to the next round," she told the two after a duet performance.

While Mays was eliminated, West ultimately made it through to the Hawaii Round and finished his run in the top five.
