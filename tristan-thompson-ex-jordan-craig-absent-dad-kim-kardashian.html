Tristan Thompson's ex Jordan Craig's sister says he's 'absent' dad to Prince &amp; rips Kim Kardashian f

KHLOE Kardashian's ex Tristan Thompson has been slammed by his first son's family for not being present in his children's lives.

The basketball player welcomed his youngest child, Tatum, via surrogate with Khloe in 2022 and is actively involved in his and his sister True's life.

On Thursday, Tristan, 32, was slammed by the sister of Jordan Craig – the mother of his oldest child, six-year-old Prince – according to TMZ.

The woman, named Kai, spoke out against the NBA star on social media, accusing him of only showing up for the children he has with Khloe, 39.

In a lengthy Instagram Story, she wrote: "It has been so hard to refrain from speaking up, and out of respect for my sister's privacy I haven't for over 7 years, but this is just too much.

"It's so painful to see how Tristan can find the time to do these nice things for others but can't seem to show up and be a real parent for my nephew Prince."

Kai continued: "The fact that he can take other children to school and activities in the same city, yet never sees or speaks to his own son, unless it's for a party or planned photo opportunity, is appalling and inexcusable."

She then tagged Khloe's sister Kim Kardashian, writing: "@kimkardashian, I don't want to believe that you are this insensitive to the fact that he literally ignores his children. That's nice if he's been a good friend to you, but let's consider our sisters before taking to global platforms to defend this man's character."

Kai then addressed rumors that Tristan pays child support to Jordan for Prince.

"On another note, to set the record straight, the child support rumors are INACCURATE! Tristan has not paid that child support in a very long time and has stopped paying Prince's school tuition," she wrote.

"Yet he's being applauded for picking-up/dropping-off other kids to school/activities. He hasn't even inquired where Prince goes to school now."

Kai continued: "Despite it all, my sister continues to work multiple jobs as she has since she was 16 years old, so that Prince will never have to feel a difference. But regardless, stepping up for your son is not all about money it's about the time spent.

"@RealTristan13 you are NOT a good father if you can't be a good father to ALL of your children."

In addition to Prince and his two children with Khloe, Tristan is a father to a baby boy that he welcomed with Maralee Nichols.

The child is the result of an affair he had while he was still with Khloe.

The Hulu star learned of the pregnancy shortly after she and Tristan agreed to move forward with surrogacy to welcome a second child.

MONEY PROBLEMS

Jordan keeps a relatively low profile and does not typically speak out against Tristan.

In August, the athlete learned that his baby mama was fighting to keep her reported massive child support payment.

At the time, the DailyMail reported that Jordan was receiving $40,000 a month from Tristan to support their son.

Her sister now claims that's "inaccurate."

The outlet reported that Jordan was working to ensure that she would continue to receive that money.

She filed an abstract of judgment in Los Angeles on August 9.

The document is used to create a public record in case the child support isn't paid.

Although the judgment was filed, no reports surfaced aside from Jordan's sister Kai's claims online that he's ever been late on or skipped child support.

There were, however, reports that Tristan's income decreased significantly.

When the payment was decided on in 2019, Tristan's annual salary was said to be $17.7million.

It dropped to a reported $9.2million.

He did not address the money drama.

HEFTY PRICE TAG

Jordan isn't the only one Tristan is supposed to be paying child support to, however.

In 2022, it was reported that he was battling with Maralee in court as she demanded $47,000 a month from the Canadian-born athlete.

At the time, Family law expert Bryan Konoski told The Sun that he expected that Tristan was already shelling out $40,000 a month to Khloe for their daughter True.

He anticipated the athlete would have to do the same for their son, who hadn't yet been born.

In December of that year, it was reported that he was ordered to pay Maralee $9,500 each month in child support for her son Theo according to TMZ.

The outlet reported that he would likely have to one day pay more but was not on a team or earning income at the time.
