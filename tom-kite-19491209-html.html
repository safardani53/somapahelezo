Thomas Oliver Kite Jr is an American professional golfer and golf course architect. He spent 175 weeks in the top 10 of the official world golf rankings from 1989 to 1994. Kite was born in McKinney, Texas. He began playing golf at the age of six and won his first tournament at the age of 11. Kite attended the University of Texas on a golf scholarship and was coached by Harvey Penick. He turned professional in 1972 and has been making money steadily ever since. Known for his innovation, he was the first to add a third wedge to his pocket, one of the first players to bring in a sports psychologist and one of the first. emphasis on fitness for the purpose of improving the game. He also underwent laser eye surgery, due to being partially blind, in an attempt to improve his game towards the end of his career. He has scored 19 victories in the PGA Tour, including the 1992 US Open at Pebble Beach. He has competed on seven Ryder Cup teams and was captain in 1997
