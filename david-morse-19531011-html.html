David Morse (TV Actor) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

David Bowditch Morse is an American actor, singer, director and writer. He first gained national attention as Dr. Jack "Boomer" Morrison in the medical drama St. Mary's. Elsewhere. He continued his film career with roles in The Negotiator, Contact, The Green Mile, Dancer in the Dark, Disturbia, The Long Kiss Goodnight, The Rock and 12 Monkeys. In 2006, Morse had a recurring role as Detective Michael Tritter in the House medical television series, in which he received an Emmy nomination. He starred as George Washington in HBO's John Adams miniseries in 2008, earning him a second nomination. He was also acclaimed for his portrayal of Uncle Peck in the Off-Broadway play How I Learned to Drive, winning the Drama Award and the Obie Award. He was also successful on Broadway, as James "Sharky" Harkin in The Seafarer. From 2010 to 2013, he starred as Terry Colson, an honest cop in a corrupt New Orleans police department, in the HBO series Treme.
