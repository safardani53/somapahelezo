Anderson Silva Career 

Anderson Silva began his professional fighting career in 1997. He lost his first professional match but then won nine consecutive fights.

Silva’s victory over Hayato Sakurai in 2001 earned him a spot in the Pride Fighting Championship, which is located in Japan.

Anderson acquired a contract with the UFC in 2006, and he made a remarkable debut against Chris Leben with a spectacular knockout victory just 49 seconds into the bout.

However, he went on to win the middleweight belt after defeating strong opponents like Rich Franklin.

In 2013, Silva was defeated for the first time by Chris Weidman. The rematch went horribly for the fighter as well, with the Brazilian suffering fractures in his left leg after Weidman kicked him out.

After his recovery, he returned to the UFC in 2015 and won his first bout, although he was suspended after testing positive for several prohibited drugs.

Silva’s career ended in 2019 after losses to Israel Adesanya and Jared Cannonier.

Anderson’s bouts have sold nearly 10 million PPV tickets; thus, Anderson Silva’s net worth is among the highest.
