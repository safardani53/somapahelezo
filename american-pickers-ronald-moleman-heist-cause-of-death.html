AMERICAN Pickers star Ronald “Moleman” Heist's cause of death has been revealed after the show guest died “unexpectantly” earlier this month. 

Ronald passed away “peacefully in his sleep” on March 7, 2023, at age 72. 

The Butler County Coroner's Office in Pennsylvania exclusively confirmed to The U.S. Sun that Ronald died of cardiac arrhythmia, due to arthroscopic cardiovascular disease.

Ronald appeared on an episode of the History Channel Show eight years ago.

During the episode, host Mike Wolfe and now-fired star Frank Fritz traveled to Pennsylvania to see his treasures.

They arrived at his home, trudging through things piled all the way to the ceiling and crossing over a one-by-two board to get to his front door.

Ronald revealed to them his vast collection, which spanned many square feet and boasted a moat and a spring.

Mike and Frank were shocked to learn that the Moleman had brought everything on the property there on his motorcycle with intention.

The show's longtime host Mike said during the episode: "He's a really cool dude."

'COLORFUL AND INTERESTING LIFE'

According to Ronald’s obituary, he is known for his “great sense of humor."

The obituary read: “Ronald had a very colorful and interesting life (and that's putting it mildly!) Purple, lime green, orange, pink and yellow; the colors were far from mellow, he said ‘the brighter, the better!’ But, when it came to songs you better ‘Paint it Black!’ Anybody that knew Ron, they know what we're talkin' about!

“Ron loved his oldies music and could name a tune in one note. A faithful caller to the oldies radio stations song request shows they all knew Ronald well. We will miss hearing, ‘this one is for Ronald.’

“Ron had a unique personality, a genius in his own way, a child at heart. He loved building, he could look at a house and precisely recreate it with Legos or City Blocks; building was his passion. He was a prankster, when he pulled a good one, he had a twinkle in his eye and had that cute sheepish grin going on. 

“He treasured his many friends and they thought the world of him. He helped them out and they helped him. We thank all of those friends who made Ronald's life a memorable one; there was never a dull moment with Ronald, there was always an adventure to be had!”

The obituary ended: “Hey Heaven, it's Ronald's calling! As Ron would say, ‘You Ain't Seen Nothing Yet!’”

The family asked loved ones to wear bright colors for the visitation and to prepare adventure stories that would “make him smile.”

ANOTHER LOSS

This isn't the first American Pickers guest star that has passed recently.

Back in 2022, Mike, 58, shared a heart-wrenching post about the death of a friend who appeared on the show.

The reality star took to his Instagram page to share some kind words about a man named Bob Peterson.

Alongside a clip from Bob's appearance on the show, Mike wrote: "A few days ago we lost a man that we looked to as a mentor. Not just with mechanical things, but in how we should live our lives. 

"Bob was an example to us as someone that loved life and seized each day with so much passion. Always there to help with such conviction and of course humor. He was from an era that we can only romanticize about now."

He concluded: "My life and so many others lives were better with him in it… He will be greatly missed. I love you my friend."

FRANK'S HEALTH BATTLE

Bob's passing came just months after Frank found himself in the hospital dealing with serious medical issues of his own.

Frank, 59, was hospitalized for a stroke on July 14 after a friend found him on the floor of his Iowa home.

Frank’s friends filed an emergency appointment of temporary guardian and conservator for the star on August 18, claiming his “decision­-making capacity is so impaired.”

He has been in and out of the hospital since with seizures and pneumonia.
