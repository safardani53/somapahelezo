Man drives for almost three miles with BODY hanging half out of his car windscreen

A MOTORIST is being quizzed on why he drove for almost THREE MILES with the body of a pedestrian sticking out of his WINDSCREEN.

The driver is understood to have told police in the US state of Kentucky that the man had apparently been walking on the main interstate road when his car slammed in to him.

He flew through the motorist's windscreen on the passenger side, but incredibly, instead of braking, the driver said he decided to "drive somewhere safer" before stopping - despite the corpse remaining half in and half out of the window.

The victim's severed leg was also left on the roadway.

Two miles down the road the driver finally pulled over at a petrol station.

Cops are now investigating the horrific accident that killed the man on the I75, reports local news station WCPO.

A witness who saw the body protruding from the car windscreen says she couldn't understand what the driver was thinking driving to the Speedway service station.

"If it had been me, I would have been jumping out of the car just running, screaming. I didn't understand his nonchalance," said Shawna Hudson.

She went on: "We just happened to get behind him when he had his flashers on and noticed that something was hanging from his car just flapping around, hitting against the ground."

She told the news channel she initially thought it was some plastic sheeting or a rug.

"I said, 'That can't be real. That has to be a sick joke,'" said Hudson.

Police found one of man's legs in the northbound lanes of the motorway about 150 yards from Kyles Lane exit.

They think the man was walking along the highway there when the car struck him.

Fort Wright dispatchers received a call just before 1am about a pedestrian in that area, but officers didn't see anybody when they responded.

Police have not yet released the names of the victim or the driver.
