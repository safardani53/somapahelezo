David Bowie smiled through the pain during last public appearance days before he died of cancer aged

DAVID Bowie died just days after his 69th birthday following a courageous 18-month battle with cancer.

The music legend passed away surrounded by loved ones on Sunday as final pictures of Bowie appearing in public for the last time emerged.

The star was snapped looking gaunt but smiling as he went to the opening night of his stage show Lazarus on December 7 - a month before he died.

Haunting lyrics on one of his final songs - which shared the same name as his musical and was released only days before he died - also appeared to show
the pop icon may have known the album might have been his last.

A track on his last album, called Lazarus and the video for which showed Bowie in a hospital bed, contained the lyrics: "Look up here, I'm in heaven."

Bowie's producer Tony Visconti said the song was a "parting gift" from the legend.

The sad news of the music icon's death was confirmed on his Facebook page this morning.

The post read: “David Bowie died peacefully today surrounded by his family after a courageous 18 month battle with cancer.

“While many of you will share in this loss, we ask that you respect the family’s privacy during their time of grief.”

His devastated supermodel wife Iman - who married Bowie in 1992 - appeared to make reference to her husband's death in a series of pictures uploaded on her Instagram page.

She posted a picture featuring the message: "The struggle is real, but so is God."

Bowie - who is survived by his wife Iman - was famed for hits including Space Oddity, Heroes and Life on Mars.

The singer's son Duncan Jones - known as Zowie Bowie - also confirmed the tragic news.

He wrote on Twitter: "Very sorry and sad to say it's true.

"I'll be offline for a while. Love to all."

Bowie, born David Jones, in Brixton, south London, released his latest album Blackstar on Friday, on his own birthday.

The singer's ex-wife Angie Bowie is currently a contestant on Celebrity Big Brother.

Stars have paid tribute to the musician after the tragic news emerged this morning.

Comedian Ricky Gervais, who convinced his long-time idol Bowie to star in 2006 sitcom Extras, wrote simply: "I just lost a hero. RIP David Bowie."

PM David Cameron wrote: "I grew up listening to and watching the pop genius David Bowie.

"He was a master of re-invention, who kept getting it right. A huge loss."

The star's producer Tony Visconti said: "He always did what he wanted to do. And he wanted to do it his way and he wanted to do it the best way.

"His death was no different from his life - a work of art.

"He was an extraordinary man, full of love and life. He will always be with us. For now, it is appropriate to cry."

The Archbishop of Canterbury also paid tribute. He said: "I remember sitting listening to his songs endlessly... relishing the impact that he had."

Kanye West wrote: "David Bowie was one of my most important inspirations, so fearless, so creative, he gave us magic for a lifetime."

Boris Johnson said: "Terrible news to hear Brixton born David Bowie has died. No one in our age has better deserved to be called a genius."

Chancellor George Osborne said: "So sad to hear about the great David
Bowie - his music was a backdrop to my life. An incredible icon of British creativity who made us proud."

Former Number 10 spin doctor Alastair Campbell tweeted a memory about Bowie and a former Prime Minister.

He said: "Only two times I saw Tony Blair star struck were when he met
David Bowie at Brits and Barbra Streisand in a make up room. Star goes out RIP."

Brit astronaut Tim Peake - who is currently in space - wrote: "Saddened to hear David Bowie has lost his battle with cancer - his music was an inspiration to many."

Tony Blair described himself as "a huge fan" of Bowie.

The former prime minister, a keen guitarist, said in a statement: "I am so sorry to hear the news of David Bowie's death. I was a huge fan.

"From the time I saw his Ziggy Stardust concert as a student, I thought
he was a brilliant artist and an exciting and interesting human being.

"It was a great privilege when I got to meet him later in life. My thoughts are with his family and friends. He will be deeply mourned."

Top Gun actor Val Kilmer offered his own passionate tribute on Twitter, saying: "David Bowie is a searing genius who will inspire millions for
thousands of years. Planets will be named after him."

Lucy Mecklenburgh said: "RIP David Bowie thoughts are with his family and friends."

The hashtags 'David Bowie' and 'Ashes to Ashes' - a reference to Bowie's hit 1980s song - were both trending on Twitter as fans expressed their shock at the star's death.

Bowie - whose latest album has already been critically acclaimed - made a surprise comeback in 2013 when he suddenly released a new single on his 66th birthday with an album out just weeks later, his first for 10 years.

The star, who grew up in Bromley, south London, kicked off his music career in the R&B boom of the early Sixties - first making it in the charts with 1969 hit Space Oddity.

A string of albums followed, before 1972's The Rise And Fall Of Ziggy Stardust And The Spiders From Mars made him an international star.

The 1980s saw him combine his pop career with appearances in films including Merry Christmas, Mr Lawrence and Absolute Beginners.

The Starman hitmaker is credited with being instrumental in the rise of glam rock and also dabbled in a wide range of other genres, including punk, electronica, rock, jungle, techno, dance and soul.

His last tour came with the 2003-2004 Reality tour and his final live performance was in 2006.
