CUT ABOVE THE REST We’re gardening experts, there's a time you should stop cutting your grass ahead of winter - and there's not long to wait

WITH winter just around the corner, keen gardeners are looking for ways to ensure their outside space continues to thrive in the cold weather. 

And there’s nothing more important than caring for your lawn and giving it enough TLC that it continues to look and feel great despite the often harsh conditions.

Luckily, gardening experts have shared that there’s one way you can help achieve that – and it’s all to do with when you should stop cutting your lawn. 

According to the Royal Horticultural Society, now is the time to start changing how often you head out with the lawn mower.

While in the summer it is recommended to be a twice weekly job, autumn sees that drop to once a week. 

But it’s when winter begins to creep in that things really change.

They explain: “Mowing is usually not necessary, unless the weather is mild and the grass is still growing. 

“In this case, mow occasionally with a high cut setting. 

“Do not attempt to do this if the ground conditions are very soft or frozen, or during spells of cold, drying winds”.

Lawnsmith.co.uk pinpoint the exact time frame to put a halt to cutting the grass is October to November.

According to one of their articles, October means “mowing will all but finish so raise the cut to winter height.

While November is “more than likely the last regular cut of the year.”

They add: “If you have a rotary mower, use it on a dry lawn to suck up autumn leaves.”

Finally, website Tom's Guide also agrees that late November should be the date for your diary. 

They explain: “By stopping mowing at the right time, you'll allow your lawn to recover over the season and encourage healthy growth for springtime.”

They went on to say: “According to experts, you should stop mowing your lawn once grass enters dormancy and temperatures drop below 50˚F. 

“For most regions, this will usually be late October or during November.
