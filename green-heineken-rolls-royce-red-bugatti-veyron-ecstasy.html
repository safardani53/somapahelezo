What are Green Heineken, Red Bugatti Veyron and Rolls Royce? Heres what we know about the ecstasy

THE deaths of two party-goers who attended the Mutiny festival are being blamed on a rogue batch of ecstasy pills.

Let's take a look at some of the dangerous tablets which have caused deaths at festivals in recent years.

What names are being given to ecstasy-type tablets?

Georgia Jones, 18, and Tommy Cowan, 20, died at the festival in Portsmouth in May 2018.

Festival-goers have described people taking "Green Heineken" pills before the horror unfolded.

Rectangular in shape, the illegal tablets are green in colour and have the word Heineken on them.

They are in no way associated with the beer giant which shares the same name.

In October 2016, Morgan Miller Smith died after collapsing at a Halloween festival in Conwy, North Wales, and is believed to have taken a pink ecstasy tablet stamped with the Rolls Royce logo.

Morgan, 16, is believed to have taken a pink ecstasy tablet with a Rolls Royce logo stamped into it.

It follows the death of teenager Shellie Callaghan in Scotland earlier the same month.

The incident led police to issue a warning urging people to stay away from pills known as Purple Ninja Turtles or Red Bugatti Veyrons.

As with the Rolls Royce pills, Red Bugatti Veyrons are stamped with a Bugatti logo and Purple Ninja Turtles appear in the shape of cartoon characters from Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles.

What exactly are the pills?

A friend of Shellie’s spoke out saying that she feared more teenagers would perish as picking up lethal tablets is easy in her hometown and they can be bought for “almost nothing”.

Professor Philip Murphy from Edge Hill University has been researching ecstasy for nearly 20 years and explained that the increasing strength of ecstasy tablets is a growing concern.

He said: “In the 1990s and 2000s the average MDMA content of an ecstasy tablet was between 50 and 80 milligrams.

“At present the average is around 125 milligrams, with pills containing 270 to 340 milligrams also being reported.

“In my opinion, this shows the importance of emphasising to potential users that a black market supply will inevitably have an element of 'Russian roulette’ and that such supplies should be avoided.”

He explained that the pills can be difficult to track as they are often differently branded.

Professor Philip said: “It is common for the tablets to have idiosyncratic logos, and it has been known for between 150 and 200 new brand logos to appear in the course of a calendar year.

“This can make it more difficult for the authorities to track the nature and origins of supplies.”

What have police said about the tablets and what should you do if you've taken one?

Police have issued a warnings to members of the public urging them to “seek urgent medical attention” if they’ve taken the tablets and not to take them at all if they have any.

A Police Scotland spokesman said: “Enquiries into the circumstances surrounding her death are at a very early stage, however one line of enquiry officers are following is that she may have had access to Ecstasy type tablets.

“These tablets are known as Red Bugatti Veyron and Purple Ninja Turtle.

“Anyone who has taken these tablets is urged to seek immediate medical attention. Furthermore, anyone who has access to any of these tablets is strongly urged not to take them.

“Police Scotland’s message is clear.  There is no safe illegal drug and no safe way to take illegal drugs.”
