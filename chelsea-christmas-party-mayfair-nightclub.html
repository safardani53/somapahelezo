Chelsea flops enjoyed Christmas party hours after Everton humiliation in posh Mayfair restaurant and

CHELSEA players still went ahead with their Christmas party on Sunday despite it coming just hours after their defeat to Everton.

The Telegraph report that captain Reece James decided to still go ahead with the social gathering for team bonding purposes.

Chelsea players headed straight from Merseyside to Mayfair where they dined at Bagatelle.

They then headed to exclusive Soho night club Tape.

Maurico Pochettino reportedly gave his blessing for his players to go ahead with the celebrations - feeling it unnecessary to cancel it in the aftermath of the disappointing defeat to Everton.

James had already requested Pochettino's permission for the night out as a Christmas party but changed the nature of it to a team bonding session in light of their latest disappointing result.

Chelsea bosses are understood to recognise the need for the team to develop their personal relationships off the field.

And Pochettino feels that is an important aspect too, and thinks the night out was a positive.

Bagatelle is one of Mayfair's most expensive restaurants where a pasta dish will set you back £48.

A salad will cost as much as £25 and the menu even has its own section for caviar.

Chelsea players could commiserate their result with 125 grams of Kristal caviar for £720.

Or if they preferred the cheaper option, they could opt for Beluga for £420.

Tape nightclub is a favourite among sportsmen and celebrities, with the likes of Logan Paul, Usain Bolt, Lewish Hamilton and Shakira all frequenting the Soho venue.

Tape is guest list only, where booking a table comes with a minimum spend of £2000.

Chelsea will be hoping the team bonding session will help them get a result at home to Sheffield United on Saturday.

The Telegraph's report claims that Pochettino believes the club need to sign a striker in January but the board are reluctant to break their newly imposed wage structure by signing the likes of Victor Osimhen or Ivan Toney.

The board recognise that the club have had a "disaster" of a year and will review their current strategy.
