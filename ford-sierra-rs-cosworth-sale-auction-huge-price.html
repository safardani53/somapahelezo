Fully restored Ford Sierra RS Cosworth up for sale at auction and set to reach HUGE price

A CLASSIC Ford Sierra RS Cosworth that has been fully restored is up for auction and set to fetch a mammoth price.

The 1987 ‘Cossie’ has 115,000 miles on the clock, and is expected to create a lot of interest among fast Ford fans.

This example is a UK-supplied, right-hand drive car finished in Moonstone Blue Metallic paintwork, with the correct-spec 15-inch ‘Lattice’ alloy wheels. 

The first owner kept the iconic motor for 15 years and covered 90,000 miles - including various road trips from the UK to France.

More recently, the car has been treated to a comprehensive restoration by Voycetune, with lots of photos showing the detailed refurbishment.

Collecting Cars said: “​​The Ford Sierra RS Cosworth is a beloved 1980s hot hatch, which achieved remarkable success in motorsport…”

“This particular car was thoroughly enjoyed by its first owner, and more recently was treated to a meticulous restoration. 

“With its compelling history and remarkable condition, this would be a credit to any collection of fast Fords or modern classic icons.”

The Sierra RS Cosworth was the first Ford to wear ‘Cosworth’ badging, and was launched as a homologation model for motor racing. 

It was also the first production car to generate over 100bhp per litre.

Under the bonnet is a 204bhp 2.0-litre turbocharged petrol engine, with a five-speed manual gearbox.

This particular RS Cosworth appeared on the cover of Classic Ford magazine in July 2010, and has also been used for various Cosworth buying guides. 

Collecting Cars remarked: “Accompanying the car is the handbook, RS Cosworth supplement, operating guide, sound system instructions, stamped service record, dealer directory, and even original 1980s press material about the Sierra RS Cosworth.  

“There is also a huge history folder of paperwork, documenting old MOT certificates as well as a raft of period invoices and receipts.”

The auction closes on Sunday 21st May, with the latest bid at the time of writing being £42,000.
