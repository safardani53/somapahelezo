Washington quarter: how much is it worth? Everything you need to know explained

IF you happen to have a few Washington quarters lying around your home – you’re probably wondering if they are worth anything.

The first Washington quarter was struck by the US Mint in 1932 – replacing the Standing Liberty design.

Like with most coins, the value could be determined by low mintage or a unique element such as an error.

Another factor is grade and condition.

We explain the Washington quarter below and how it could potentially be worth thousands if you happen to come across the right piece.

What is the Washington quarter? 

You might be familiar with two designs of the Washington quarter in particular.

Some designs of the coin include the text “Liberty” or “United States of America” over Washington’s head. 

There are also the Washington state quarters, which all feature their own unique designs on the reverse.

The quarters have been minted in Denver, Philadelphia, and San Francisco.

You can spot them on the coin by looking for a D, P, or S letter mark, which can be found in the bottom right of the obverse or the bottom middle of the reverse – depending on the design.

Also, keep in mind, that some coins minted in Philadelphia do not feature letter marks.

The most valuable Washington pieces in circulation include two coins with the dates 1932 – which are the only ones in the series with mintages of under 1million.

These are known as the 1932-S and 1932-D pieces, which have a respective mintage of 408,000 and 436,800.

“Both coins have been highly sought after in all grades for the past 60 years,” David Hall, former President of Professional Coin Grading Services wrote in a description of the coin.

“They are the key dates of the Washington quarter series and actually are two of the better known rare coins of the twentieth century.”

The 1936-D piece could also be valuable. 

While its reported mintage of more than 5.3million isn’t low, Mr Hall noted that not many coins were saved during the 1936 issue.

Additionally, Washington error quarters could be valuable as well.

How much are they worth?

If you happen to spot a rare coin, grade and condition could dictate how much it’s worth.

For example, the 1932-D and S quarters are both worth $200 in average condition, according to USA Coin Book.

However, the D quarter could be worth up to $13,910, while the S quarter’s maximum value is only $5,545 in Mint State (MS) 65 condition, which is an uncirculated grade.

The 1936-D coin is worth $25 on average, but it can go as high as $1,228 if in an MS 65 grade, according to USA Coin Book. 

In a recent online listing, one sold for $247.

As far as error pieces go, one state quarter sold for $216, and a double-struck piece sold for $218.

Also, it’s worth noting that a 1954-D Washington quarter graded in “MS 67+” by Numismatic Guaranty Company (NGC) sold for more than $2,200 in an eBay auction this month. 

How to find rare coins

There are many ways you can find rare coins.

The first thing you’ll want to do is check places in your home or any places you might store change.

Some other things you can try are using a metal detector in known areas for buried treasure and by stopping by your local bank branch and asking for a wrapper of pennies.

You can also try to get a good deal on one online through USA Coin Book or eBay.

But make sure you research the coin first and know what it’s worth before buying.

And also - be aware of potential fakes online.

To avoid this, it's worth checking the seller’s history and whether or not the coin was certified in the listing.

Your quarters aren’t the only things that might hold value, as nickels, dimes, half dollars, and Lincoln pennies are selling for thousands online.
