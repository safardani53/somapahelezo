Billie Eilish strips to her bra to make bold bodyshaming statement on start of her world tour Th

BILLIE Eilish stripped down to her bra to make a bold bodyshaming statement in Miami on the first night of her Where Do We Go world tour.

The 18-year-old superstar was seen taking off her top in a video that played in the background, while she discussed her battle to overcome everyone's "disapproval" over body image.

In the two-minute clip, she could be seen slowly unzipping her top as her voiceover said: "I feel your stares, your disapproval."

She then took her top off completely, while discussing the pressures of being a young female artist in a sexualized industry.

"If I wear what is comfortable, I am not a woman. If I shed the layers, I'm a s**t," she said.

"Though you've never seen my body, you still judge it, and judge me for it."


Billie Eilish's anti-bodyshaming statement in full

You have opinions about my opinions,
about my music, about my clothes, about my body.
Some people hate what I wear, some people praise it,
some people use it to shame others,
some people use it to shame me.

But I feel you watching, always,
and nothing I do goes unseen.
So while I feel your stares, your disapproval,
or your sigh of relief, if I lived by them,
I'd never be able to move.
Would you like me to be smaller?
Weaker? Softer? Taller?
Would you like me to be quiet?
Do my shoulders provoke you?
Does my chest? Am I my stomach? My hips?
The body I was born with, is it not what you wanted?

If I wear what is comfortable,
I am not a woman.
If I shed the layers, I'm a s**t.
Though you've never seen my body, you still judge it and judge me for it.
Why?

We make assumptions about people,
based on their size.
We decide who they are,
we decide what they're worth.
If I wear more, if I wear less,
who decides what that makes me?
What that means?
If my value is based only on your perception?
Or is your opinion of me not my responsibility?"

Billie started the show with the track Bury A Friend.

She gave an energetic performance which saw her sprawling around on the floor, before crawling to the other side of the stage.

The star later clambered up to the higher tiers of the venue to sit on the railings, hanging her legs over the side.

Fans were treated to a bumper show with a total of 22 songs - but were left waiting until song 21 for epic hit, Bad Guy. She rounded off with track Goodbye.
