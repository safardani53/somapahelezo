Woman completely revamps her VERY 90s red kitchen tiles in one afternoon using just a 16 paint

WHEN it comes to home improvements, there's no denying that the kitchen is one of the most expensive areas to have professionally redecorated.

Well if you're feeling fed up with your kitchen tiles, one mum has proved that you don't need to rip them out to give the room a whole new look.

Posting on the Facebook group Mrs Hinch Made Me Do It, the savvy DIY fan revealed how she gave the subway tiles a new lease of life with a single pot of paint.

She wrote: "Wanted to get rid of the red tiles as they were here when we bought the house and I just couldn’t work with the red!"

Instead of splashing out on new tiles, the mum decided to simply redecorate them and bought a £16.99 pot of Johnstone's tile paint.

In a matter of hours, this simple and quick paint job made the mum's VERY dated kitchen looked bang on trend.

And the best part is, she didn't need to use an undercoat - and simply buffed them and applied the paint with a sponge roller.

The mum shared the before-and-after photos on the group to ask other members if she should use a darker grout pen - but was convinced to leave it as it is.

One replied: "It looks perfect!

"Thanks for sharing that this can be done."
