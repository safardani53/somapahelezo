Joe Flacco Bio: Wife, Kids &amp; Net Worth!

Joe Flacco is a name that resonates with NFL fans worldwide, and for good reason. Flacco started playing football early and quickly developed a talent for the sport.

He played college football at Delaware as a quarterback, eventually catching NFL scouts’ attention.

In 2008, Flacco was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in the first round of the NFL Draft, marking the beginning of an impressive professional career.

Over the years, Flacco has become known for his strong arm, accurate passes, and ability to lead his team to victory. He played a pivotal role in the Ravens’ Super Bowl XLVII win, earning himself the prestigious title of Super Bowl MVP.

Despite his many achievements, Flacco’s future in the NFL is currently uncertain.

After playing for teams such as the Baltimore Ravens, Denver Broncos, New York Jets, and Philadelphia Eagles, Flacco is currently a free agent, and it remains to be seen where he will end up next.

While he played for the New York Jets in the 2022 season, his contract expired, and he was not re-signed, leaving fans wondering what’s next for this talented player.

Early Life & Family

Joseph Flacco, also known as Joe Flacco, was born on January 16, 1985, in Audubon, New Jersey. He was raised in a family of six children, being the eldest of five sons and having one younger sister.

His parents, Stephen Flacco and Karen Flacco, originally from Haddon Township, New Jersey, moved to Audubon when Joe was three years old.

Sporting Family

Joe Flacco’s family has a strong athletic background, with his father having played football and baseball for the University of Pennsylvania.

Likewise, Joe’s brothers also excelled in sports, playing football and baseball in college and beyond. Mike, Joe’s younger brother, was drafted by the Baltimore Orioles in the 2009 MLB Draft as a first baseman.

John, another younger brother of Joe, played quarterback for Stanford University and Towson University.

Brian played baseball for the New Jersey Institute of Technology, while Tom played quarterback for Rutgers University and Towson University.

However, Stephanie, the youngest of the Flacco siblings, has kept a low profile and is not widely known to the public. Joe’s family has always supported his career, and he often attends games.

Education & Early Career

Joe grew up playing football, baseball, and basketball at Audubon High School, where he was the starting quarterback for the Green Wave.

The player then went to the University of Pittsburgh in 2003 and played for the Pittsburgh Panthers football team.

Joe transferred to the University of Delaware in 2006 and led the Delaware Fightin’ Blue Hens football team to a win in 2007. 

Career

Joseph Flacco is an American football quarterback who has played for five different teams in the NFL throughout his 14-year career.

The player began his career with the Baltimore Ravens and led them to a Super Bowl victory in 2013, where he was named the MVP.

He has also played for the Denver Broncos, New York Jets, and Philadelphia Eagles. He has accumulated over 42,000 passing yards, 232 passing touchdowns, and 147 interceptions.

The player holds several records, including most passing yards and touchdowns for the Ravens, and has the most road playoff wins by a quarterback in NFL history.

One of his most notable performances was in the 2012 AFC Championship Game. At AFC,  the player threw for three touchdowns in the second half to lead the Ravens to a victory over the New England Patriots and a spot in the Super Bowl.

Wife & Kids

Joe Flacco is married to his high school sweetheart, Dana Grady. The couple got married in 2011 and has been together ever since.

Joe and his wife, Dana, have five children together. They welcomed their first child, Stephen, in June 2012, followed by their second son, Daniel, born on September 15, 2013.

In January 2015, they welcomed their third child and another son named Francis, and in September 2016, they had their one and only daughter, Evelyn.

Their fourth son, Thomas, was born in April 2018, completing their family.

It’s worth noting that Daniel’s birth was hours before Flacco played a game, and Flacco skipped the Pro Bowl in 2015 to be with Dana when she gave birth to Francis.

Dana takes care of their children and is a dedicated housewife, allowing Flacco to focus on his football career.

Despite his busy schedule, Flacco always makes time for his family and puts them first, showing his dedication on and off the field.

Charity

Joe Flacco has been actively involved in various charitable causes throughout his career. While with the Baltimore Ravens, he donated to multiple charities, including Children’s Miracle Network Hospitals and the Baltimore Humane Society.

In 2013, he partnered with Pizza Hut to raise funds for the World Food Programme and delivered pizzas to fans who ordered online.

The player also supported the Special Olympics Maryland by participating in their annual Polar Bear Plunge event, which aims to raise awareness and funds for the organization.

In 2014, he joined forces with Pampers and March of Dimes to promote healthy pregnancies and babies in celebration of Father’s Day.

Net Worth & Contract

Joe Flacco has amassed a considerable net worth of around $90 million as of November 2023, thanks to his successful career as a quarterback in the NFL.

He has earned over $165 million in salary throughout his career, which includes a record-breaking six-year contract worth $120.6 million with the Ravens.

Flacco has also made significant money through endorsement deals with popular brands like Nike, Pepsi, and McDonald’s.

In March 2022, Joe signed a one-year contract with the New York Jets worth $3.5 million, which included a signing bonus of $1.73 million and $2.85 million guaranteed at signing.

However, the contract has expired, and he is currently an unrestricted free agent.
