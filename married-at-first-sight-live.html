Comparing the US show to the UK

Married at First Sight is also on British and Australian airwaves.

The concepts are the same but the UK version differs slightly from the US.

In the UK, the cast is larger and instead of recruiting from the same city, members from all over the country participate in the show.

The UK also skips preliminary episodes following the contestants’ reactions to being on the show and begins the season with the wedding.

The UK also includes LGBTQ+ couples and participants who have been married before or have children, unlike in the US.

MaFS origins in the US

Married at First Sight first premiered on July 8, 2014.

It has since had 16 seasons.

The program featured three couples for the first seven seasons, MaFS has had four or more couples since season eight.

The show follows six or more people who simultaneously meet and walk down the aisle.

By the end of each season, the cast members are tasked with deciding to stay together or divorce.

Recap from Decision Day: Shaquille and Kirsten

Kirsten told Shaquille on Decision Day that throughout their marriage he had multiple things to focus on and it took a toll on their relationship but through it all he supported her and she wants to stay married.

Shaquille said he appreciated both of them for not giving up on each other and that Kirsten is loving and kind but it’s been very difficult for him to stand up and be the man for her.

He tearfully asked for a divorce, shocking Kirsten and the experts.

She handled the rejection without confrontation but expressed in her confessional that she was shocked by Shaq’s decision.

Kirsten slammed her ex-husband for crying when he was the one asking for a divorce.

They met up at a park to get closure but Shaq didn’t seem happy with how the conversation ended.

Recap from Decision Day: Clint and Gina

Clint and Gina decided to go their separate ways on Decision Day.

Clint said Gina is great and would do this again with her as his wife but ultimately thinks it’s best if they get a divorce.

Gina agreed with Clint and thinks it’s best for the couple to get a divorce.

The two are moving on with other people as they both went on dates after Decision Day.

Gina went for coffee with Mack and Clint kissed Dom in a shocking dare last week.

Recap from Decision Day: Airris and Jasmine

Airris and Jasmine both mutually agreed they should get a divorce.

Jasmine told Airris that she knows what she wants and learned even more about what she desired in marriage from being with him.

Airris said the marriage hasn’t gone the way either of them planned and said he would like a divorce as well.

In an earlier episode, Airris admitted he was physical with another woman after Decision Day and Jasmine went out with a mystery man.

However, the two reunited and seemed to be maintaining a healthy friendship.

Recap from Decision Day: Chris and Nicole

Nicole and Chris are the only couple still married after Decision Day.

Nicole told Chris in an earlier episode that he has made her the happiest she’s ever been in 33 years of her life.

Chris responded that he expected marriage to have lots of ups and downs and they might still have their downs but she makes life fun for him and the two agreed to stay married.

They stayed together in the episodes after Decision Day but fans will find out tonight how their marriage fared after the cameras stopped rolling.
