Ziaire Williams Bio: Contracts &amp; Relationships

Ziaire Williams is a young American basketball player who plays for the Memphis Grizzlies of the NBA. The NBA player was trained under the guidance of his father when he was just eight years old. 

Williams was a straight five-star recruit and the number one forward in the 2020 recruiting class.

His collegiate debut was for Stanford on November 30, 2020, scoring 19 points and eight rebounds in an 82-64 win against Alabama.

On February 2, 2023, the rookie player was initially held questionable due to right knee soreness but received the green light ahead of the contest.

Williams, with an average of 7.7 points and 2.3 rebounds across 22 minutes in the last six appearances, is one of the most promising basketball players in the world.

Early Life, Family, & Education

Ziaire Williams was born in Lancaster, California, on September 12, 2001. He is the only child of Ziaire Williams, his father, and Marquita Fields Williams, his mother.  

Both his parents served in the military service. His father served in the Marine Corps, and his mother was in the Army and Air Force. Currently, the player’s father works for 7 Up in Los Angels, and the mother is a probation officer. 

High School Education

Ziaire Williams went to Notre Dame High School for the first three of his school, scoring 13.6 points and 6.5 rebounds per game in his freshman year.

However, he missed much of his sophomore season due to a knee injury. For his senior year, on July 18, 2019, William transferred to Sierra Canyon School in Chatsworth, Los Angeles.

He was on the team with fellow five-star recruit BJ Boston, Bronny James, and Zaire Wade.

College

William choose Stanford over Arizona, USC, North Carolina, and UCLA for his further studies and career path.

He had a 10.7-point, 4.6 rebounds, and 2.2 assists per game average as a rookie while shooting 37.4 percent from the field.

On March 31, he announced his intention to skip his remaining college eligibility and enter the 2021 NBA Draft.

NBA Career 

On July 29, 2021, the New Orleans Pelicans picked him as their tenth overall pick but traded him to the Memphis Grizzlies.

Unfortunately, he missed 14 consecutive matches due to an ankle injury and Covid 19 protocols. However, on January 6, 2022, Williams got his first career start scoring 14 points and winning over Pistons. 

Moreover, on February 2, the rookie player set a career-high with 21 points scored in a 120-108 win over the New York Knicks.
